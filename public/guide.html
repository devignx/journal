How Lapse works

A journal your AI writes for you. Here's how to get the most out of it.

three ways to log

  • Talk to your AI. Say “log this: shipped the redesign today” in Claude, ChatGPT, or any connected client. It writes the entry in your words, tagged and timestamped.
  • Quick capture. An iPhone Shortcut (or any device) POSTs straight to your journal — dictate on the move, no app to open.
  • Write it here. The box at the top of the feed logs directly to the current space.

Every entry remembers where it came from — you'll see a small via AI / via capture label.

spaces

Entries live in spaces — separate areas of your life: Journal, Career, Philosophy, Legacy, whatever you name. Switch with the dropdown next to the logo; your AI can log to a named space (“add to my career space”) and create new ones on the fly.

tags & typed tags

Tags link entries together. Beyond plain tags like work or health, Lapse understands typed tags — metadata that names the things in your life:

personasha projectlapse placegoa moodlow topicstoicism

Write them as type:value. They become nodes in your graph — so “everyone I mention when I'm stressed” or “every entry about a project” becomes answerable. Your AI reuses the exact values it already knows, so the vocabulary stays clean.

the graph

Open Graph from the account menu to see your tags as a network — nodes sized by how often you use them, linked when they appear together. Filter by space at the top. It's a map of your themes: what clusters, what connects, what's drifted apart.

rituals your AI can run

  • log_today — a couple of quick questions, then it journals your day.
  • weekly_review — reads the week, reflects patterns back, offers to save the reflection.
  • remember_this — resurfaces a random past entry.

These show up in the connector's prompt menu. You can also just ask: “what did I do last week?”, “when did I last mention Asha?”, “am I on a streak?”.

editing

Hover any entry to edit or delete it. Your AI can update and delete too — but it always confirms before removing anything.

your data

Everything is scoped to your account and invisible to others. Login is passwordless — a one-time email link. Access tokens are stored hashed, never in plaintext. It's your journal; treat it as your legacy.
